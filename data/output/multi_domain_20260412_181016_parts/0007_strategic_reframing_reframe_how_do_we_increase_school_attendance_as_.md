## Iteration 1

### 🔍 Curiosity

**Hidden Assumptions:**
- Attendance is solely a behavioral issue
- Improved attendance equals improved academic outcomes
- The root causes of absenteeism are external to the school system

**Unexplored Domains:**
- Public health infrastructure integration (mental/physical)
- Local economic development cycles
- Community parental empowerment structures

**Key Questions:**
- **What if 'attendance' is a proxy metric for a deeper, unmeasured resource need (e.g., nutrition, stability)?**
  *Shifts focus from compliance to underlying support systems*
- **How does the definition of a 'successful student day' change if the goal is skill acquisition over seat-time metrics?**
  *Challenges the temporal rigidity of the school day*
- **Which stakeholders (parents, employers, healthcare providers) have the most vested interest in 'attendance' besides the school itself?**
  *Identifies non-educational levers for systemic change*
- **What systemic mechanisms currently punish non-attendance (e.g., failure, disciplinary action) versus those that reward consistent engagement?**
  *Exposes the negative feedback loops governing the system*

**Branch Seeds:** Success Definition 1: Zero chronic absenteeism (Public Health Metric) · Success Definition 2: 90% mastery of core curriculum objectives (Academic Metric) · Success Definition 3: Student self-reported sense of belonging/connection to school (Social-Emotional Metric)

### 🌿 Creativity

**Research:**
- Tiered intervention models (wraparound services)
- Predictive analytics for truancy risk
- Incentive-based attendance programs (rewards)

**Branches:**
- **B1 — Public Health Systems Integration:** Mandatory community health screening linked to truancy waivers, Micro-insurance models for family stability gaps
- **B2 — Skill-Gap Curriculum Redesign:** Modular, competency-based credits earned via non-classroom engagement, Curriculum pacing based on documented skill mastery, not calendar time
- **B3 — Stakeholder Accountability Network:** Local employers sponsoring 'Re-engagement Apprenticeships' for absent students, Primary care physicians flagging attendance gaps as health alerts
- **B4 — Behavioral Economics Gamification:** Reputation/Status system for consistent, varied engagement (not just physical presence), Token economy rewarding proactive participation across domains
- **B5 — Systemic Failure Diagnosis:** Mandatory cross-referencing of school records with local housing/utility status, Automated trigger for social worker deployment based on pattern deviation

**Pruned:**
