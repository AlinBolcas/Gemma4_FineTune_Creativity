## Vanilla

That's a wonderfully minimalist pantry! With just **flour, butter, and eggs**, you have the foundational ingredients for some classic, simple, and delicious baked goods.

Since you don't have any leavening agents (like baking powder or soda) or sugar, your options will lean towards savory or very simple, unsweetened preparations.

Here are several things you can make, categorized by complexity:

---

## 🍳🍳🍳 Savory & Quick Bites (Easiest)

These are perfect for a quick snack or side dish.

### 1. Simple Pancakes and Crepes (The Classic)
This is your most versatile option.
* **How:** Whisk the eggs. Gradually whisk in the flour until you have a smooth, thin batter. Melt the butter in a lightly oiled pan. Pour small amounts of batter into the hot pan to form thin pancakes or larger crepes.
* **Best For:** Savory toppings (like a little salt, pepper, or if you happen to have some herbs), or just eating plain.

### 2. Flatbreads / Quick Dough
This requires a bit more kneading/kneading, but is very satisfying.
* **How:** Mix the flour, a pinch of salt (if you have it), and a little cold butter until it forms a shaggy dough. Add the eggs to bind it, then knead until smooth. Roll out very thin and cook in a hot, dry pan until golden brown on both sides.
* **Best For:** Dipping (if you have water/oil), or serving alongside something else.

### 3. Simple Frittata/Omelet Base (If you treat the egg as the main item)
If you treat the eggs as the star, you can use the flour to slightly thicken them.
* **How:** Whisk the eggs vigorously. Stir in a small amount of flour to give it some body. Melt the butter in a pan, pour in the egg mixture, and cook slowly until set.
* **Best For:** A very plain, rustic egg dish.

## 🥞🥞🥞 Baked Good Foundation (Requires more patience)

These items will have a more substantial structure, but they will be plain (no sweetness).

### . Basic Biscuits/Scones (If you can achieve a flaky texture)
If you work the cold butter into the flour mixture *before* adding the eggs, you can mimic a scone texture.
* **How:** Cut the cold butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the eggs and mix *just* until the dough comes together. Pat it into a disk, cut out shapes, and bake until risen and golden.
* **Note:** Without leavening, these will be more like dense, sturdy sconescotch rather than fluffy American biscuits, but they will be edible!

## Tuned (scale 1.0x, temp 0.8)

This is a classic, wonderfully limited pantry challenge! With just **flour, butter, and 2 eggs**, you can make several delicious, comforting, and fundamentally simple things.

Here are ideas categorized by effort level and style:

---

## 🍳🍳🍳 **Super Easy (Minimal Shaping)**

These require almost no technique, just mixing and cooking.

1. **Simple Pancakes & Crepes (The Fastest)**
    * **How:** Whisk flour, eggs, melted butter until smooth. Stir in just enough water or milk substitute if you have it (if truly *only* these three, it will be very thick like a batter for thick pancakes).
    * **Cook:** Melt butter in a skillet. Pour batter and cook until bubbles form, flip.
    * **Serve:** Plain, or with a tiny pat of butter on top.

2. **Simple Flatbreads (Quick Griddle)**
    * **How:** Mix flour, softened butter, one egg, and a pinch of salt (if available) into a soft dough. Knead lightly.
    * **Cook:** Roll out thinly and cook on a hot, dry skillet until puffed and lightly browned spots appear.
    * **Serve:** Warm, folded, or torn into pieces.

3. **Basic Scrambled Eggs with Fluffy Dollops**
    * **How:** Whisk the two eggs with a tablespoon of melted butter. Stir in a tablespoon of flour to give the eggs body and prevent them from drying out too much.
    * **Cook:** Melt butter in a non-stick pan over low heat. Pour eggs and gently push them from the edges toward the center until mostly set.
    * **Serve:** As is, or folded over your flatbreads.

---

## 🔪🔪🔪 **Medium Effort (Shaping & Resting)**

These require a short rest period for the dough/batter to relax.

4. **Basic Biscuits or Scones (Quick Rise)**
    * **How:** Cut cold butter into flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Cut in the eggs and mix just until a shaggy dough forms. Do not overmix.
    * **Shape:** Pat into a round or square and cut with a fluted cutter (or a knife).
    * **Bake:** Bake in a preheated oven until golden brown.
    * **Serve:** Warm with butter.

5. **Simple Drop Cookies (No Chill Required)**
    * **How:** Cream butter with the two eggs until light. Gradually beat in the flour. Drop rounded spoonful-sized dollops onto a lined baking sheet.
    * **Bake:** Bake until edges are set and centers are lightly puffed.
    * **Serve:** Fresh from the oven.
