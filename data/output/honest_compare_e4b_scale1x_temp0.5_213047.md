## Vanilla

That's an exciting challenge! To create a "wow" factor for judges at a hackathon, you need a project that isn't just functional, but **impactful, innovative,,and technically impressive.**

"Wow" usually comes from one of three places: **Scale/Impact, Novelty/Creativity, or Technical Depth.**

Since you are using Gemma 4 (a powerful LLM), you should lean into what LLMs do best: **understanding complex information, generating creative output, and automating cognitive tasks.**

Here is a spectrum of ideas, categorized by the type of "wow" they deliver, ranging from highly practical to wildly ambitious.

## 💡💡 IMPACT & SOCIAL GOOD (The "Heart" Wow)

Judges love projects that solve real, visible problems. These show you care about the world.

1. **Hyper-Local Policy Summarizer (Civic Tech):**
    * **The Problem:** Local government documents (zoning laws, city council minutes, public health advisories) are dense, jargon-heavy, and inaccessible.
    * **The Solution:** An LLM interface where a user inputs a topic (e.g., "bike lane regulations in District 4"). Gemma processes the relevant city documents and generates a **plain-language, bulleted summary** tailored to a specific audience (e.g., "For a renter," "For a small business owner").
    * **🔥WOW Factor:** Direct, measurable impact on civic engagement.

2. **Ethical Bias Auditor for Content:**
    * **The Problem:** LLMs can perpetuate biases present in their training data.
    * **The Solution:** A tool where a user inputs a piece of generated text (or a prompt they are about to use). Gemma analyzes the text against a predefined set of ethical guidelines (gender representation, cultural sensitivity, political neutrality) and provides a **"Bias Score"** with actionable suggestions for rewriting problematic phrases.
    * **🔥WOW Factor:** Addresses the biggest current ethical challenge in AI.

3. **Personalized Learning Path Generator (Adaptive Tutoring):**
    * **The Problem:** Standardized learning materials don't account for individual knowledge gaps or learning styles.
    * **The Solution:** The user inputs their current skill level and a desired outcome (e.g., "Learn Python enough to build a basic web scraper"). Gemma generates a **dynamic, multi-stage curriculum**, creating custom quizzes, analogies tailored to the user's stated interests (e.g., "Explain recursion using basketball analogies"), and progressively harder tasks.
    * **🔥WOW Factor:** Demonstrates deep personalization, moving beyond simple Q&A.
