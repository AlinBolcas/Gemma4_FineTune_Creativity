## 💡💡💡 Category 1: The "Deep Impact" Wow (Social Good & Ethics)

These projects tackle a significant, visible problem and show how AI can provide a tangible solution.

1. **AI-Powered Local Resource Navigator:**
    * **The Problem:** People in underserved communities often don't know where to find specific resources (food banks, mental health services, job training, free clinics).
    * **The Solution:** A conversational AI (powered by Gemma) that ingests local, often unstructured data (community flyers, NGO websites) and provides hyper-personalized, real-time routing and eligibility checks.
    * **The "Wow" Factor:** It moves beyond a simple database search to *understand* the user's complex need ("I have two kids, low income, and need dental care near the subway").

2. **Bias Auditor for Public Datasets:**
    * **The Problem:** Many open-source datasets used to train AI contain hidden biases (racial, gender, socioeconomic) that lead to unfair AI outcomes.
    * **The Solution:** A tool where users can upload a dataset, and Gemma analyzes the distribution, flags statistical imbalances, and suggests targeted augmentation or re-weighting strategies to mitigate bias *before* training a model.
    * **The "Wow" Factor:** It addresses the *meta-problem* of AI ethics—fixing the data itself.

3. **Micro-Learning Tutor for Critical Skills:**
    * **The Problem:** Traditional education is slow; people need quick, targeted help on specific, high-stakes skills (e.g, "How to write a persuasive grant proposal," or "Understanding basic circuit diagrams").
    * **The Solution:** A generative AI that acts as a Socratic tutor. Instead of giving the answer, it asks guiding questions based on the user's incorrect answer, forcing them to learn the underlying concept.
    * **The "Wow" Factor:** It demonstrates advanced conversational flow and pedagogical understanding, not just information retrieval.
